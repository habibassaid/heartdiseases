Protecting yourself from a heart attack involves adopting a healthy lifestyle and managing risk factors. Here are some key measures you can take to reduce the risk of heart attacks:


1-Maintain a Healthy Diet: Follow a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Limit the intake of saturated and trans fats, sodium, and added sugars.
2-Exercise Regularly: Engage in physical activity for at least 150 minutes per week. Incorporate aerobic exercises like brisk walking, cycling, or swimming, as well as strength training exercises.
3-Control Blood Pressure: Monitor your blood pressure regularly and take steps to keep it within a healthy range. If necessary, follow your healthcare provider's recommendations for blood pressure management.
4-Manage Cholesterol Levels: Maintain healthy cholesterol levels by consuming a low-cholesterol diet, limiting saturated and trans fats, and, if needed, taking prescribed medications as directed by your healthcare provider
5-Regular Check-ups: Visit your healthcare provider for routine check-ups and screenings. Regular monitoring of your health can help identify and manage risk factors for heart disease.
